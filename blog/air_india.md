# Flying Air India (Again)

I flew Air India for the first time in over 10 years. It was everything I expected — unfortunately, not in a positive sense.

## Booking Chaos

This was a last-minute trip: a friend’s wedding in a non-metro city in Maharashtra. With limited options, I booked Air India via Delhi.

Twenty hours before departure, I received an email:

> Your flight has been rescheduled. Your new layover time is **-5 minutes**

Yes — a negative layover.

It is difficult to understand how such an itinerary is generated, let alone sent to customers.

Calling customer support did not help. The issue was acknowledged, but no meaningful alternatives were offered. The only option presented was a full refund.

Shortly after, a second email arrived: I had been rebooked onto a flight the following day. My original 3-hour layover had become 21 hours — meaning I would miss the wedding entirely.

At this point, I contacted the booking agent. They quickly rebooked me onto an earlier flight, preserving the original connection. Crisis averted — but notably, this solution did not come from the airline itself.

## Pre-Departure

Boarding was uneventful. We settled in, relieved to have resolved the scheduling issues, and looking forward to catching up on sleep.

That optimism did not last long.

## The Flight

As we taxied, the pilot announced a technical issue. We returned to the gate.

For the next two hours, updates followed a predictable pattern:

> “We have rebooted the system, but it has not worked.”

There is a certain irony in applying standard IT troubleshooting techniques to an aircraft. Eventually, after multiple attempts, the issue was deemed resolved — or at least acceptable for departure.

Shortly after takeoff, water began dripping from the overhead compartment onto my friend. We chose not to escalate it, partly out of fatigue and partly out of scepticism that it would help.

Thirty minutes later, we were informed that the in-flight entertainment system was not functioning, likely a consequence of the earlier “reboots”. A long nine-hour flight became longer.

## Arrival (Almost)

The remainder of the flight was uneventful.

We landed in Delhi and managed to navigate the connection without major issues, though not without minor inconveniences — including a gate change and temporarily missing flight information.

At our final destination, one last surprise awaited: a wet suitcase. Likely left out in the rain during loading, it had soaked through the top layer — unfortunately, where my wedding clothes were packed.

Not ideal, but recoverable. With some quick preparation, I made it to the ceremony on time — slightly damp, but presentable.

## Reflection

Air India has undergone significant structural changes, including privatisation and consolidation. There is reason to be optimistic about its long-term trajectory.

However, based on this experience, there is still a considerable gap between intent and execution.

Perhaps I’ll try again in another ten years.
